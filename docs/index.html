What is a herrenlos parcel?

Under Swiss law (ZGB Art. 664 and Art. 964), most land in Switzerland has a registered owner. A small fraction does not — either because the owner gave it up (Dereliktion) or because the parcel was never entered in the Grundbuch in the first place.

This site surveys every cantonal land registry that's publicly accessible and flags parcels that match the legal pattern of herrenlos. Owner names come from the registries cited above (public under ZGB Art. 970 §2); the parcel geometry comes from the federal cadastral survey.
